Schengen Visa

A Schengen visa allows you to stay in the Schengen Area for up to 90 days within any 180-day rolling period, with some countries having additional requirements.

For example, in France, you must register with the local authorities within 3 days of arrival, while in Italy, you must obtain a residence permit if you plan to stay for more than 90 days (according to Italian Immigration Law, Article 5). As of 2024, the Schengen Area has introduced a new visa policy, allowing for longer stays in certain countries, such as Spain, which now allows stays of up to 183 days for digital nomads (source: Spanish Immigration Act). In comparison, other countries like the Netherlands have stricter rules, with a maximum stay of 90 days within any 180-day period (per Dutch Immigration Act).
