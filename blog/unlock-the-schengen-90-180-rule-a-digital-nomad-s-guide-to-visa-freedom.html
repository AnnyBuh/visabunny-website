How Does the Schengen 90/180 Day Rule Work?

The Schengen 90/180 day rule allows non-EU citizens to stay in the Schengen Area for up to 90 days within any 180-day rolling period, with the 180-day period moving forward with each passing day.

To illustrate this, let's consider an example: a US citizen arriving in Germany on January 1st, 2023, can stay for up to 90 days, until March 31st, 2023. However, if they leave the Schengen Area on February 15th, 2023, and return on April 1st, 2023, their new 180-day period will start from April 1st, 2023, not from January 1st, 2023. This means that their previous stay from January 1st to February 15th will be counted towards their new 180-day period, affecting their remaining days.
